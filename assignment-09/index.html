Brick Stitch for Beginners


You will need:

K/O or NYMO B thread
Size 11/0 Delica beads
Size 12/0 beading needle
beading mat

Let's get started!

  1. Measure out a 4ft piece of thread (usually a wingspan's length is just fine).
  2. Use the ladder stitch method to make a row of 10 beads.
  3. To begin each row of brick stitch, pick up two beads. Place them down towards the ladder and pass the needle through the connecting thread of the first two beads in the ladder. Pull snugly.
  4. Pass the needle back up through the second bead on the second row. Pull tight.
  5. For the rest of the row, pick up one bead to make each brick stitch. Stitch under the connecting thread "bridge" under each new bead. Put needle in from the back of your piece through the front.
  6. Stitch back up thorugh the bead you just added and pull snugly. Continue this pattern to create your second row!
  7. At the end of your second row, you will begin to work on your third row in the opposite direction.
  8. Pick up two beads (as in step three) and follow steps four through six to create your next row.
  9. Repeat steps two through seven until your piece is the size you would like.
  10. Complete your piece with a few half hitch knots. Try to weave the thread into the center of the beadwork to tie your knots.
  11. Put a dab of glue on your knots (use a toothpick for precision) and voila! You have yourself a brick stitch piece!
